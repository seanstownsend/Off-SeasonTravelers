Day 1: Lower Loop Introduction

Old Faithful, Grand Prismatic, and Yellowstone Lake

9:00 AM - 10:30 AM

Old Faithful Geyser

Start your Yellowstone adventure at the park's most famous geyser. Erupts approximately every 90 minutes. Visit the visitor center for prediction times.

[Photo: Old Faithful eruption with fall colors]

Off-season advantage: Virtually no crowds at viewing areas!

11:00 AM - 12:30 PM

Grand Prismatic Spring

America's largest hot spring with stunning rainbow colors. Take the boardwalk around the spring and hike to the overlook for aerial views.

[Photo: Grand Prismatic Spring aerial view]

Pro tip: Steam is more visible in cool off-season air!

2:00 PM - 4:00 PM

Yellowstone Lake

Visit the largest high-elevation lake in North America. Drive along the shoreline and stop at West Thumb Geyser Basin.

[Photo: Yellowstone Lake with mountains]

Wildlife watch: Look for trumpeter swans and osprey.

Day 2: Grand Canyon of Yellowstone

Artist Point, Lower Falls, and Uncle Tom's Trail

8:00 AM - 10:00 AM

Artist Point

The most famous viewpoint in Yellowstone. Marvel at the Lower Falls of the Yellowstone River plunging 308 feet into the colorful canyon.

[Photo: Lower Falls from Artist Point in fall]

Photography tip: Best light is morning for falls photography.

10:30 AM - 12:00 PM

Uncle Tom's Trail

Steep trail with 328 metal steps leading to a platform with spectacular views of Lower Falls. Moderate difficulty.

[Photo: Uncle Tom's Trail viewpoint]

Difficulty: Moderate - stairs can be icy in off-season.

2:00 PM - 4:00 PM

Hayden Valley Wildlife

Prime wildlife viewing area. Look for bison herds, elk, and potentially bears. Bring binoculars and be patient.

[Photo: Bison herd in Hayden Valley]

Best time: Early morning and late afternoon for wildlife activity.

Driving distance: ~45 miles, 1.5 hours total

Day 2 Tips

  • Check trail conditions - some may be icy in off-season
  • Bring bear spray when hiking any trails
  • Pack warm clothes for early morning wildlife viewing
  • Download offline maps - cell service is limited

Day 3: Mammoth and Tower-Roosevelt

Hot springs terraces and Lamar Valley wildlife

8:00 AM - 10:00 AM

Mammoth Hot Springs

Explore the limestone terraces formed by hot springs. Walk both the Upper and Lower Terrace boardwalks for different perspectives.

[Photo: Mammoth Hot Springs terraces in autumn]

Unique feature: Terraces change daily as mineral deposits shift.

11:00 AM - 1:00 PM

Lamar Valley

Known as "America's Serengeti" for its abundant wildlife. Drive slowly and scan for wolves, bears, elk, and bison.

[Photo: Wolf pack in Lamar Valley]

Wildlife tip: Bring binoculars or spotting scope. Rangers often set up scopes for public use.

2:30 PM - 4:00 PM

Tower Fall

132-foot waterfall surrounded by volcanic pinnacles. Easy walk to viewpoint, or hike down for closer views.

[Photo: Tower Fall with volcanic rock formations]

Historical note: Named by the Washburn Expedition in 1870.

Wildlife Safety Reminder

Stay at least 100 yards from bears and wolves, 25 yards from elk and bison. Off-season wildlife can be more unpredictable as they prepare for winter or emerge from winter dormancy.

Day 4: Hidden Gems and Favorites

Revisit favorites and explore lesser-known spots

9:00 AM - 11:00 AM

Norris Geyser Basin

Hottest thermal area in Yellowstone. Explore both Porcelain Basin and Back Basin boardwalks. Home to Steamboat Geyser.

[Photo: Norris Geyser Basin steaming vents]

Thermal feature: Temperatures can exceed 200Â°F just below the surface.

12:00 PM - 2:00 PM

Fairy Falls Trail

Easy 2.4-mile hike to a beautiful 200-foot waterfall. Trail also provides great views of Grand Prismatic from above.

[Photo: Fairy Falls with autumn foliage]

Hiking tip: Trail may have snow in early spring/late fall - check conditions.

3:00 PM - 5:00 PM

Return to Favorites

Use this time to revisit your favorite spots from the previous days. Perfect for better photos, deeper exploration, or relaxation.

[Photo: Sunset at Yellowstone Lake]

Photography: Golden hour light perfect for landscape photography.

Day 4 Flexible Options

  • If weather is poor, spend time in visitor centers and museums
  • Consider a ranger-led program or evening presentation
  • Shop for souvenirs at park stores
  • Relax at your accommodation and plan your next adventure

Day 5 (Optional): Extended Exploration

For those with extra time - deeper dives and nearby attractions

Morning Option

Grand Teton National Park

If staying extra, drive south to Grand Teton National Park (1 hour). Perfect for dramatic mountain photography and different scenery.

[Photo: Grand Teton mountains reflection]

Day trip option: Jenny Lake and Jackson Lake areas are accessible year-round.

Alternative

Beartooth Highway

One of America's most scenic drives (when open). High-altitude route with stunning mountain vistas. Check seasonal closures.

[Photo: Beartooth Highway scenic overlook]

Note: Usually closed October-May due to snow. Check current conditions.
